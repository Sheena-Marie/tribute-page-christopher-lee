Christopher Lee

A Tribute

Early Life & Military Service

Christopher Lee (CBE) was born in Belgravia, Westminster, London, on May 27, 1922. Born Christopher Frank Carandini Lee, we was the son of Colonel Geoffrey Trollope Lee (a member of the 60th Kings Royal Rifle Corps) and Countess Estelle Marie (neé Carandini di Sarzano). He also had one sister, Xandra Carandini Lee.

When World War Two broke out, he volunteered for military service. He was originally going to become a pilot, but was diagnosed with a failed optic nerve not long before his first solo flight and was told he would never fly again.. After this, he applied for the RAF Intelligence. He was attached to the Special Operations Executive and the Long Range Desert Group but never really spoke about it.

Acting Career

His acting career covered nearly 70 years, starting in 1957 and he was still active until not long before his death. He had signed on to star in a Danish film a month before he died in 2015

Some of his most famous roles:

  • 1957 - 1976: He had multiple roles with Hammer Film Productions
    • His most famous Hammer role was Dracula.
  • 1974: He played Francisco Scaramanga in James Bond: The Man with the Golden Gun.
  • 2002 -2006: Count Dooku in Star Wars.
  • 2001 - 2003: Saruman in The Lord of the Rings
  • 2011: He returned to the role of Saruman for The Hobbit: The Unexpected Journey

He also did a lot of voice work for multiple projects, such as returning to voice Scaramanga in the Goldeneye video game and narrating The Hobbit Lego game.

He was knighted in 2009 for services to drama and charity.

Music

He was also involved in the music scene. He performed on The Wicker Man soundtrack as well as many other albums. In 2005 he was introduced to heavy metal when he sang a duet with Fabio Lione. In 2010, he released his own metal album Charlemagne: By the Sword and the Cross. He announced a new metal single on his 90th birthday. His final musical appearnace was as a narrator on a track for the Hollywood Vampires, which was recorded not long before he died.
